Juvenile Crime in 2017

The conversation around criminal justice and criminality has reached a new peak as a new genre in infotainment now includes a popular subgenre encompassing crime. Podcasts such as "My Favorite Murder" and Netflix shows such as "Making a Murderer" have received critical and cultural acclaim forcing lawmakers and voters to take new looks at our justice system. That being said, the spotlight has not yet reached minors who commit felonies. In 2017, the FBI found that 634,535 out of 8,247, 591 (7.6%) felonies are committed by those under age. Juvenile Crime in 2000

While the above number might seem large, it is less than a quarter of the arrests made in 2000. According to the National Center for Juvenile Justice, 2.3 million minors were arrested almost two decades ago. This is a difficult number to grasp, but is important to grapple with as we continue to seek rehabilitative and educational sanctions for criminal behavior.

Now What?

What can we attribute the declining rates of juvenile crime to? The Juvenile Justice Information Exchange seems to think it could be due to "more attention being paid to child welfare." Claims that increasingly realistic violent video games are affecting juvenile crime cannot hold up against the declining trend. While this - thankfully - declining trend continues, the Juvenile Justice Information Exchange still urges people to consider that black and Latinx children still make up a disproportionate number of juvenile inmates.
